Overlapping grids

Overlapping grids are used to efficiently discretize PDEs in complex geometries. Consider a rectangular geometry with an interior hole. An overlapping grid configuration will look like the following.

There are two grids, a background grid and a boundary fitted grid. These two grids communicate at interpolation points (squares). On this grid, I am solving the Poisson equation. For verification purposes, an exact solution is used to test the code.

Since the solver is second-order accurate, the errors are expected to reduce by a factor of four with each grid doubling, as seen. I also reported timings for each stage of the algorithm. Since Matlab is used, the longest stage is the assembly of the discretization matrix.
